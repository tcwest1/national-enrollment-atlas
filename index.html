    Methodology & data notes

    Sources: NCES Common Core of Data (CCD) school directory and membership files, 2016–17 through 2024–25, and NCES EDGE-derived school district and school point boundary files (2024–25 geography applied across all years).

    Scope: 50 states + DC. American Samoa, Guam, the Northern Mariana Islands, the U.S. Virgin Islands, the Bureau of Indian Education, and DoDEA domestic schools are present in the source data but not yet mapped here.

    Join keys: LEAID/NCESSCH in the directory and membership files are zero-padded to 7 and 12 digits respectively to match the GEOID fields used in the boundary files.

    No boundary on file: many charter and other non-geographic LEAs (their own LEAID, no district catchment) have no EDGE boundary polygon. These are plotted as a small marker at the average location of their schools and flagged in lists rather than silently dropped.

    Color domain: wider at finer grain since smaller bases swing further — national ±12%, within-state districts ±25%, within-district schools ±40%. An entity open only in the later year is flagged new; one open only in the earlier year is flagged closed/merged, rather than assigned a misleading rate.

    Geography: state outlines come from the public us-atlas TopoJSON CDN. District and school boundaries/points come from the local EDGE-derived files in this repository.

    No enrollment data leaves the browser — all files are fetched directly from this repository.
