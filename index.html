Cohort survival ratios and enrollments used to produce 2025-2026 projections

Grade-level enrollments for 2021–2022, 2022–2023, 2023–2024, and 2024–2025 are shown first, followed by the 2024–2025 three-year average cohort survival ratio. Ratios are displayed as numeric values with two decimal places.

Method

Local files: The app expects school directory files by school year and state in json/directory/, such as json/directory/ccd_sch_directory_2425_NM.json, and school membership files by school year and state in json/membership/, such as json/membership/ccd_sch_membership_2425_NM.json.

Enrollment: After a district is selected, the app loads the local CCD school membership file for each school year/state, filters to the selected LEAID, and sums K–12 membership. Users can view the full district total or switch to an individual school within the selected district.

Aggregation: District mode aggregates school-level membership rows to the selected LEAID. School mode filters to both the selected LEAID and selected school identifier, then sums grades K–12 only. Pre-K, grade 13, ungraded, and adult education are excluded when grade fields are available.

Cohort-survival forecast: The dotted projection line sums projected grade-level enrollments. For grades 1–12, the survival rate is enrollment in grade g, year t, divided by enrollment in grade g-1, year t-1, averaged over the three most recent available transitions. Kindergarten and other entry grades at the selected analysis level do not use a survival rate; the latest enrollment for those entry grades is carried forward. This matters for individual schools such as middle schools and high schools, where the first served grade does not have a prior grade inside the same school. It is intended as a quick planning signal, not an official projection.

Projection years after 2025–2026: The 2025–2026 projection is calculated from the actual 2024–2025 grade-level enrollments and the three-year average cohort survival ratios. For 2026–2027 and later years, the tool no longer uses additional CCD actual enrollment data. Instead, each future year is calculated from the prior year’s projected grade-level enrollments using the same cohort survival ratios. As a result, projected increases or decreases can compound over time.

Cohort survival ratio table: The table shows grade-level enrollments for 2021–2022 through 2024–2025 and the 2024–2025 three-year average cohort survival ratio by grade level. For grades 1–12, each ratio compares enrollment in grade g in year t to enrollment in grade g-1 in year t-1, averaged across the three most recent available transitions ending in 2024–2025. Kindergarten and entry grades without a prior grade are shown as entry grades rather than survival ratios.
